Tour Cozumel

Playa El Cielo

Scenic, serene white-sand beach with clear water popular for snorkeling & photography. Visiting Playa El Cielo won’t leave you disappointed. Located on the southern end of Cozumel, Playa El Cielo is a relaxation paradise.

One of the more popular beaches in Cozumel, you can take in all the aspects of this tropical beach. Relax on the warm white sand or take a dip in the warm water and go exploring below the surface.

There are many other beaches to visit, but Playa El Cielo on one of the premium beaches that Cozumel has to offer. Rated a five-star on travel sites, this beach will take you away to the oasis that you’ve always wanted to experience. This beach is family-friendly with plenty of space to spread out and relax in the sun.

Chankanaab National Park

Chankannb was made a national park in 1980 and has been a place of refuge and protection for fauna and flora and is home to dolphins, manatees, and sea turtles.

Its name comes from the Mayan language which means “little sea”. The name comes because of the natural lagoon located inside the park and is surrounded by ruins of ancient Mayan culture and civilization. It is located within the National Marine Park of Cozumel at kilometer 9 of the coastal road south of Cozumel.

At the Chankanaab beach adventure park, you can spend the day either snorkeling or diving, while admiring tropical fish, colorful reefs, underwater sculptures, and caverns. You can also visit the crocodile farm, check out the sea lions, or even get a massage.

One of the most popular things to do is to experience swimming with the dolphins, offered by Dolphin Discovery, or take a swim in their lagoon and explore the wonders under the sea.

About Cozumel

Cozumel, is a mostly undeveloped Mexican island in the Caribbean Sea, nestled just 12 miles off the coast of the Yucatan Peninsula. Cozumel is Mexico's largest island and a popular cruise ship destination and is famous for its white sandy beaches and to scuba dive into the remarkable clarity of the multi-hued azure Caribbean ocean in which it resides.

Cozumel measures 28 miles long and 10 miles wide. The climate is subtropical and the people native to Cozumel are of Mayan descent.
